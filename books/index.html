Books

Books always have been a part of my life. I love reading and always keep reading something anywhere I can: on a public transport, during mealtime or even a bathroom. It can be novel, adventure, stories.I also try to write some books too.

Three muketeers is my favourite teenage book. I love it because it's full of adventure where the heroes are the amazingly bravemen with the sense of high dignity, courage and respect. They showed a great commitment to their friendship by undertaking.
Jane Eyre is a great book about the girl who had a life-stuggling childhood and verey harsh condition to survive. It was fascinating to watch her manage to keep some self-dignity and self-esteem to herself despite all the hardships she had to go through. She showed a soul-freedom and a strong character
Chekhov's stories are amazing! They impress me with the stories about Russian aristrocrates who were highly educated showing a great sense of dignity to themselves and anything which surrounds them.
Marry Poppins is my favourite childhood book. It describes such a warm atmosphere of love of the family and misterious nanny. The most beloved parts are when the nanny showed some jaw-dropping tricks to the children leaving them with the amazement and fascination.
Gone with the wind is a novel with the story about the war and how the people needed to adapt to drastic changes of their lives which was a great challenge for them.
The lord of the ring is the most interesting novel with the most fascinating characters and landscapes.I was moved by their deeds and their behaviour during the critical situation sticking to commitment to their values.
